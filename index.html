Real question

Current approaches in neuroscience failed to help us understand a microprocessor.

- What approaches? - Why they fail?

What approaches?

  • (Anatomical) Connectomics
  • Single-cell lesion
  • Single-cell tuning
  • Correlation / Functional connectivities
  • LFP / Oscillations
  • Population activities / Dimension reduction

Why they fail?

  1. The brain is different
  2. Our approaches are inadequate

Why they fail?

  1. specify one or more (hypothetical) scenario where the analysis **in the paper** would actually help us understand the brain, despite their failure in revealing anything about the microprocessor. Then, try to summarize the critical difference between the brain and microprocessor that would make this happen.
  2. specify on or more analysis that is **not in the paper** that would help us understand how a microprocessor works, and has the potential to help us understand how other computing system works, including the brain.
